\section{Introduction}

Forecasting the future image-plane location of an observed UAV is useful for event-camera tracking, sensor scheduling, interception, and downstream recognition. Event cameras are attractive in this setting because they can capture fast apparent motion and high temporal contrast, such as propeller motion and rapid target displacement. The practical question is whether event-derived visual evidence improves short-horizon UAV box forecasting beyond a strong physical or kinematic baseline.

Our starting point is deliberately conservative. The original FRED benchmark evaluates full bounding-box forecasting, including box ADE/FDE, center ADE/FDE, and mean IoU~\cite{magrini_fred_2025}, while a recent Kalman-based follow-up focuses on center-point trajectory forecasting only~\cite{rpm_kalman_2026}. That follow-up shows that classical Kalman-style models are difficult to beat on center ADE/FDE. We therefore extend the Kalman state to full center-size boxes and study learned forecasting as a residual problem: the Kalman or last-four linear state supplies a stable box rollout, and a learned model predicts acceleration corrections.

The second issue is shortcut learning. Future acceleration is not independent of the current image position and velocity. For example, targets near image borders may tend to turn back into the frame, high-speed trajectories may decelerate, and many horizontally oriented recordings may contain a global vertical acceleration bias. A residual model can exploit these regularities even when it is not using target appearance. This matters for out-of-distribution use, where the camera orientation, operator behavior, or flight envelope may differ from the training split.

We organize the paper around three questions:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item How strong is an optimized Kalman filter when evaluated as a full box forecaster against the original FRED box metrics?
  \item Does a single event-derived image input add useful residual information beyond filtered state and box history under leakage-reducing settings such as cutouts and relative coordinates?
  \item Do residual gains persist when we reduce linear predictability of fitted acceleration from motion priors?
\end{enumerate}

Our contributions are:

\begin{itemize}
  \item a residual Kalman forecasting benchmark for FRED UAV bounding boxes, including a center-size Kalman baseline that extends center-only Kalman trajectory forecasting to width and height;
  \item an empirical analysis of how fitted future acceleration correlates with anchor position and velocity, including simple linear baselines over filtered state features;
  \item a sample-level decorrelation procedure that greedily removes forecast windows to reduce motion-prior predictability, with an optional penalty on global mean acceleration; and
  \item preliminary evidence that single-input event-conditioned residuals improve standard-split box forecasting, while decorrelated splits are a stricter test of whether those gains survive reduced motion shortcuts.
\end{itemize}

We prioritize controlled settings over a broad architecture search: fixed-size cutouts, relative box-history features, optimized Kalman state features, and a ResNet-18 encoder for each single-image experiment.
